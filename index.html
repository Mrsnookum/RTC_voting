Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about identity protection, cryptographic hashing, and registration alignment.

Is my vote truly anonymous?

Yes. The RTC portal uses decoupled data processing. When you vote, the system verifies your Admission Number in one database, but saves your actual vote in a completely separate, anonymous ledger.

What happens if I try to vote twice?

The system utilizes aggressive server-side rate limiting and status checks. Once you submit a ballot, your profile is permanently locked for the duration of the election.

What if my Admission Number doesn't work?

If you receive an "unauthorized" error, it means your details are not synced with the current active academic registry. You must contact the RTC Registrar's office to update your status.

Can I change my vote after submitting?

No. For security and integrity reasons, once you confirm and submit your ballot, the cryptographic hash is sealed and recorded. It cannot be altered or retrieved.
